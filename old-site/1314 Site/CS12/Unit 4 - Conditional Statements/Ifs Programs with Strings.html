Using if statements to determine the value of a variable:
Before you start, look at the two following if statements, which do the same thing but in different ways:

  responseName = "King Sacco";

   responseName = "lesser being";

Why is a truck $16.50 for 6 hours?
There are three zones that the time for a truck can land in: 0-1 hours, 2-4 hours, and 5+ hours.  In our sample, that means that the truck's 1st hour cost $1, the next three hours cost $7.50 (3*2.5).  This means that any truck that stays more than three hours will pay at least $7.50.  From there we have to decide how many hours past the first 4 hours that the truck has stayed.  In this case, the truck has stayed 2 more hours. Those hours cost $8.00 (2*$4.00).  In total, the first 4 hours cost $8.50, plus the $8 for the last 2 hours for a total of $16.50.

Parse the three number-based Strings into ints:
year: the last two digits of the year

Calculating the Day:

This algorithm shows how to come up with four numbers. Find the sum of these four numbers and using modulus to restrict the result to 0 through 6, the day of the week can be determined.

The four values are:

1. Calculate the century value:
centuryValue = 2(3 - (century mod 4))
when century is the first two digits of the year.

2. Calculate the year value. Because there are 365 days in a common year, which is 52 weeks plus 1 day, each year will start on the day of the week after that from the preceding year. Each leap year has of course one more day than a common year. Assuming we know on which day a century starts (from above), if we add the number of years elapsed since the start of the century, plus the number of leap years that have elapsed since the start of the century, we get the day of the week on which the year starts. Calculate this value where year is the last two digits of the year:

yearValue = year + year / 4;

3. Calculate the monthValue. We refer to the months table below to work out on which day of the week a month starts. Notice that January starts on day 0, which is simply another way of saying that the year and January of that year start on the same day.

The months table shows the value for leap years also. You’ll have to check to see if the year is a leap year (if it’s divisible by 4) in order to know what the montValue should be.

- January - 0 (in leap year 6)
- February - 3 (in leap year 2)
- March - 3
- April - 6
- May - 1
- June - 4
- July - 6
- August - 2
- September - 5
- October - 0
- November - 3
- December – 5

Hint about monthValue: One way to use else-if chains is to decide what value should go into a variable. First you declare an int monthValue, and then each part of the else if will set monthValue to a specific value, similar to the example at the top of the page.

4. Calculate the dayValue. This is easy, it's just the day of the month.





 To figure out what day of the week it is:
-Compute the sum of the four values above.
-Divide the sum calculated from the last step by 7 and find the remainder.
