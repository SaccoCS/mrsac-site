The Vibrating Problem:

 

One common problem that we can come across with our Pong method is the vibrating that can occur.

 

 

Here’s why this happens:

When a ball hits a paddle dead on, it sinks a little bit into the paddle.  Reversing the vector causes it to bounce the same distance OUT as it took to get IN the paddle.

 

 

When the ball enters the paddle through the top, it is so far into the paddle that after one step the reversed xVector isn’t enough to move all the way out.  This causes it to bounce again.

 

 

A minor fix to this is to modify your if statement to both bounce horizontally and set the x ( x = ...) to the position where it’s just touching the paddle.

 

If done correctly, the ball will have a small blip when it enters the paddle through the top of the paddle instead of the vibration.
