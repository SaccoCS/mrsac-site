Bouncing off the walls

 

Now that we have a ball that drifts across the screen, we can edit our code to create a ball that bounces when it hits the wall.  The two things we're going to have to do is check for collision against a wall, and change the vectors to bounce off of that wall.

 

1)  The Timer will be responsible for first making the ball move, then checking to see if it should bounce.  For our purposes, the ball should bounce when it goes out of bounds.  The left wall, for example, is at x = 0.  Once the x coordinate of the ball is less than zero, it is in the zone where it should bounce

 

 

Be careful on the right wall.  You have to be aware of where x and y really are on the ball.  Most people leave x and y as the upper left hand corner, which is fine, you just have to make sure that you know the right side of the ball is at x + ballWidth instead of just x.

 

For Example, if you only check to see if the x of the ball crosses over the right wall, it will bounce too late

 

 

Modify your if statements to adjust for the width of the ball.

 

 

 

 

2)  If the Timer decides that the ball should bounce, you simply need to reverse the direction of ONE of the vectors.

 

To perform a horizontal bounce you flip the xVector  (  xVect = -1*xVect; ) while a vertical bounce is the yVect.
