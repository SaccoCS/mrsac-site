DeMorgan's Law

Copy and Paste the program below into your project.  Notice that there are two versions of each method, an A and a B.  The A version takes the parameters and performs some sort of AND or OR statement, and then negates it (flips it to its opposite) right before returning.  Your job is to write a boolean statement that has exactly the same outputs, but uses DeMorgan's law to get the opposite instead of the "!".

For example:  if the boolean statement was !(  x>4 || x<0 ), the correct solution would be x<=4 && x>=0.
