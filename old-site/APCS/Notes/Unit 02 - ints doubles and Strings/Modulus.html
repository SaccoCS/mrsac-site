Modulus (mod)

In Java, the % sign stands for an operation that you don't usually use in your math class called the Modulus, or mod for short.  While the symbol and the name may be new, the concept should not.  Mathematically, the modulus operator is used for finding the remainder.

25 % 4 would be read as "25 mod 4" and the result would be 1.  This is because 4 goes into 25 four times with a remainder of 1.

Similarly 15 % 6 is a 3 because the 6 goes in twice with a remainder of 3.

 

In terms of order of operations, mod falls on the same level as multiplication

int a = 5 + 7 % 2 * 6;

In the statement above the mod is the first operation that occurs ( 7%2 --> 1).  After that the multiplication happens, and finally the addition.  The resulting value stored in the int a is an 11.

 

Good things to know about Mod:

1)  If a number is even, then modding by 2 will give a zero.  Since even numbers are divisible by two there will never be any remainder when modding by two.

2) When modding by a number, the result is always less than the number you modded by.  For example:  anyNumber%4 can only have the result 0,1,2, or 3.  Any larger and it would be enough so that another 4 could be taken out.
