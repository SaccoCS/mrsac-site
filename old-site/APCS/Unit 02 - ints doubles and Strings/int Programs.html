Easter

Write a program named Easter, which firsts asks the user to input the year, and then uses the Gaussian Algorithm to calculate the month and the day that Easter falls on.  You may output "Easter is on Month: 4 and Day: 15"

This is a very complicated algorithm with many variables.  Order of Operations is important!  You will have to add parentheses to make sure that the division happens in the right order.

If you do not get the right answer, put the following println in and test your values against the sample below.

Gauss’ algorithm for calculating Easter

Let y be the year

Divide y by 19 and call the remainder a.  Ignore the quotient

Divide y by 100 to get a quotient b and a remaiinder c

Divide b by 4 to get a quotient d and a remainder e

Divide 8*b+13 by 25 to get a quotient g.  Ignore the remainder

Divide 19*a+b-d-g+15 by 30 to get a remainder h.  Ignore the quotient

Divide c by 4 to get a quotient j and a remainder k

Divide a +11*h by 319 to get a quotient m.  Ignore the remainder

Divide 2*e+2*j - k - h + m + 32 by 7 to get a remainder r.  Ignore the quotient

Divide h – m + r + 90 by 25 to get a quotient n.  Ignore the remainder

Divide h – m +r +n +19 by 32 to get a remainder p.  Ignore the quotient

Then Easter falls on day p of month n.  For Example, if y is 2001:

a = 6
b = 20
c = 1
d = 5, e = 0
g = 6
h = 18
j = 0, k = 1
m = 0
r = 6
n = 4
p = 15

Therefore Easter Sunday fell on April 15, 2001.

 

DecToBin: 

One way to convert to binary is to use mod and int division repeatedly:

1)  Mod your number by 2.  The result is the next largest digit.
2)  Divide your number by 2 using int division.  The result is your new number.
3)  Repeat steps 1 and 2 until your number is 0.

For Example:  11

11 % 2 = 1

11%2 = 1 Binary number so far:  1
11/2 = 5 New number:  5
Repeat  
5%2 = 1 Binary number so far: 11
5/2 = 2 New number: 2
Repeat  
2%2 = 0 Binary number so far: 011
2/2 = 1 New number: 1
Repeat  
1%2 = 1 Binary number so far:  1011
1/2 = 0 New number: 0
